Our Mission

Our mission is to create a safe community platform that brings together professionals and young Africans who want to share knowledge, experiences and learn from each other. We believe in equal access to modern technology, which becomes a culture that promotes the love for quality education and innovation for the African youth.

Our Vision

A network of knowledge sharing training hubs led by passionate African technologists whose purpose is to use the power of technology to promote quality education, empower the youth and enable them to drive positive change and claim ownership for the development of Africa.

Our Journey So far

It is widely recognized on the African continent that poor education and lack of access to technology are major stumbling blocks to youth empowerment, economic competitiveness, and social equality. As feeder subjects for a career in Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM), the low quality and access to technology for youth in Africa is alarming and needs to be addressed.

Our passion

As passionate change agent scientists and technologists, we are always looking for ways to make an impact, to show children and young adults the opportunities that exist in embracing technology, inspiring the next generation of scientists and technologists who will lead Africa's contribution in producing emerging digital technologies.

The campaign

With over 10 years experience in the STEM field and leveraging from the success of the Knowledge Sharing Campaign (KSC), we decided to take action by creating the Young African Technologists (YAT) network, that is aimed at promoting education through technology for the African youth in underprivileged communities.

Knowledge Sharing Campaign (KSC)

Based on a specific background, skills and interests, everyone chooses and plays one of the Agile roles – Business Analyst (BA), Developer, Quality Analyst (QA) and UI/UX.

Bring, Share & Learn

There are no specific prerequisites to participate in these sessions. In fact, only a few students who attend these sessions have their own laptops. KSC is not just about tech sessions, but about knowledge sharing. Students who come have very limited exposure to key technologies. They first attend the KSC introductory sessions, which focus on assessing and improving the participant’s computer literacy.
